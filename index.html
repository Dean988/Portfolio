Hi, I'm Dino. A sociologist and data scientist learning to build the future, one algorithm at a time.

A Humble Approach to Technology

I don't believe in tech just for the sake of it. My background isn't just code, it's people. I started with a degree in Sociology, where I learned to really listen to communities and understand the context behind the numbers. It taught me that every algorithm eventually touches a real person's life.

When I moved into Social Data Science and AI Management, I realized the best systems are the ones that handle human complexity instead of ignoring it. I'm still learning every day. My goal isn't just to build smarter machines, but to create tools that actually help the people using them. I try to approach every project with curiosity, asking "why" we're building something before we figure out "how".

It starts with understanding people. My roots in sociology ground every technical decision I make. Before writing any code, I ask who it's for and how it affects them. This helps me see the blind spots that purely technical teams might miss.
